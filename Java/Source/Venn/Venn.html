Venn Diagrams and Probability

You need Java to see this.

Venn diagrams are a way of visualizing sets. This applet lets you hilight the set A, the set B, the universal set S, the empty set {}, the complement of A (Ac), the complement of B (Bc), the intersection of A and B (AB), the union of A and B (A or B), the intersection of the complement of A with B (AcB), and the intersection of A with the complement of B (ABc). One can think of the relative area of a set to the area of S as a probability. You can drag the events A and B around, which changes the probability of their intersection and union. When the probability of the intersection of A and B equals the product of the probability of A and the probability of B (when P(AB) = P(A)*P(B)), A and B are independent. Notice how hard it is to drag A or B around to make them independent. The scrollbars let you adjust the probability of A and the probability of B.
