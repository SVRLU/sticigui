SquARe
Squ(are) A(cceptance) Re(gion)ARe Simultaneous Confidence Sets

This applet shows the acceptance regions inverted to get SquARe confidence sets, which tend to exclude zero in more components than do traditional equivariant simultaneous confidence sets when one or more observations are small.

The acceptance regions have minimum intersection with the orthants that do not contain the parameter, and are otherwise as short as possible in each direction. Additionally, they are constrained to be at most r times as wide and high as the traditional equivariant set.
